23&me DNA Testing

I have always been very interested in history, and I love learning about the past. Also, I enjoy learning about DNA and heredity. With these two interests of mine, it was natural for me to want to do a 23&me DNA test. I wanted to learn about my own history. So, for my 16th birthday, my parents bought me a kit that tested my genetic traits and ancestry, and I sent my sample in.

When I got my results back, I was not very surprised. My ancestry results were:

  • 85.5% South Asian
  • 10.4% Middle Eastern
  • 1.8% European (Southern & Northwestern)
  • 0.6% East Asian (Southeastern & Siberian)
  • 1.8% Unassigned

Of course, I knew that I was going to be South Asian because my family is from Pakistan and parts of Northern India (before Partition), so that was a given. I just wish that 23&me had a greater sample size, so specific ethnic groups could be added because South Asia is full of extremely diverse groups of people. I knew I had Middle Eastern DNA because my maternal great-grandmother was Iranian. I suspected that I had European DNA because the British Empire had colonized South Asia for two hundred years. In my family, it has been passed on that we are direct descendants of Genghis Khan. I don't know how much credibility is in that since practically the whole continent of Asia and much of Europe is related to him. However, the Siberian DNA could be a remnant of the Mongol Empire in me. I was a bit disappointed at the 1.8% unassigned, which means that 23&me could not pinpoint exactly where some DNA segments were from because the algorithm requires more data to make my results more accurate.
